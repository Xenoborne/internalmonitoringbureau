IMB Rank Structure

CLASSIFICATION: LEVEL 2 CLEARANCE REQUIRED - Document ID: IMB-RANK-001

The Internal Monitoring Bureau maintains a hierarchical rank structure across all operational divisions. Personnel progress through ranks based on performance, experience, and operational requirements.

Rank Progression Overview

All IMB personnel begin as Recruits and must complete a probationary period before division assignment. Promotion through ranks requires demonstrated competence, loyalty verification, and recommendation from division leadership. Detailed promotion criteria are classified above Level 2 clearance.

Rank Identification

Visual Identification System

IMB personnel display rank insignia on their uniforms in the following locations:

  • Shoulder Patches: Division insignia and rank designation
  • Badge: IMB credentials with rank indicator
  • Collar Pins: Rank indicators (command staff and above)

Note: Division insignia are separate from rank insignia. Personnel wear their division badge in addition to their rank insignia.

Promotion Requirements

⚠️ CLASSIFIED INFORMATION

Specific promotion criteria, time-in-grade requirements, and evaluation procedures are classified above Level 2 clearance. Personnel seeking promotion information should consult with their direct supervisor or Division Chief.

Career Progression

IMB career progression is merit-based and performance-driven. Factors considered for promotion include:

  • Operational performance and results
  • Demonstrated loyalty and integrity
  • Security clearance level and maintenance
  • Recommendation from supervisory personnel
  • Completion of required training and certifications

Note: Division assignment and specialized roles may have additional requirements.
